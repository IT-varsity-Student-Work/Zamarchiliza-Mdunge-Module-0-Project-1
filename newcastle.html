NEWCASTLE


Newcastl is the third largest city in the province of KwaZulu Natal. Newcastle has changed names on numerous occasions during the country's historic rul. It was initially named Post Halt Number2 on the military maps during 1840s. It was later known as ther Waterfall River Township because of the Ncandu River, it was then given the name Newcastle after the British Colonial Secretary, the fifth Duke of Newcatle. During the Anglo-Boer War, Newcastle was invaded by Boer forces on 14 October 1899. The Newcastle name lasted for seven months when it was named Viljoensdorp after the commander, General Ben Viljoen. However after the relief of Ladysmith under the command of General Redver Buller, the British forces reclaim the name back to Newcastle. Newcastle is famous for its coal mining, steel and wool production.


FORT AMIEL

Newcastle is known for its rich cultural history. There are many monuments which can be assosiated to the First Boer War and the Anglo Boer War such as the Fort Amiel.The Fort Amiel is a museum foermerly a Fort built in 1876 by the 80th Staffordshire Volunteers and Major Amiels as a look out for British soldiers leading up to the Zulu War. The fort was used as a garrison foe the Kings Royal regiment during the war and it also served as a hospital, transit camp and depot. Foet Amiel is now a historical and cultural museum exhibiting two anglo Boer Wars, a reconstruction of the British Army Base and displays a large collection of item used. This interesting site is well worth a visit, there is a lot to discover and explore and it will all age groups.


NCANDU STATE FOREST NATURE RESERVE

The Ncandu State Forest Nature Reserve is the second biggest indigenous forest in KwaZulu Natal and is filled with grassy plains and yellowwood gorges. There are many little streams running throgh the forrest that meets the Ncandu River were there is plentiful. The forest is idealfor a scenic stroll or a relaxing horse ride. Many trails run through the reserve which takes you all through the sandstone cliff, to the majestic waterfalls and allow for fabulous birding opportunities. You get there by a boat or can walk on boardwalks through the forest or above the roof of the forest where the views are simply beautiful.
