PIETERMARITZBURG




Pietermaritzburg(PMB) is the capital city of the province Kwa-zulu Natal and it is the second lagest city in the province. It was found in 1833, in the Zulu language it is called umGungundlovu. Pietermaritzburg is known as the "City of Flowers" for its azaleas and roses and also because it is the site of the country's National Botanical Gardens. Pietermaritzburg also has a butterfly conservation caled Butterflies for Africa. PMB is a regionally important industrial hub, producing timber, aluminium and dairy products.Two interpretations existed about the origin of the name of the city. One is that it was named after two Voortrekker leaders Piet Ritief and Gert Maritz. The other interpretation was that it was origionally after Piet Ritief alone since hi full name was Piet Marits Retief. In this interpretation the original name was "Pieter Maurits Burg" later transliteraed to the current name.Pietermaritzburg is the home of the University of KwaZulu Natal. the University of Natal was founded in 1910 as the Natal University College and extended to Durban in 1922. The two Campuses were incorporated into the University of Natal in March 1949. It became a major voice in the struggle against apartheid and was the the first universities in the country to provide education to black students. the name was changed to the curent name on 01 January 2004.



GANDHI STATUE OF PEACE


Mahatma Gandhi spent 21 years in South Africa, eleven of them in KwaZulu Natal. After he was thrown off the train is believed to have been the time he made a life changing decision to remain in the country to resit the oppression of Indians in the counrty. At that time Gandhi was a young lawyer sent to South Africa from India on a temporary assignment on behalf of a local india trade. His active yet peaceful, resistance to political injustices imclude the writing of letters of protest and signing of mass petitions. He was a thorn in the side of the colonial government. Gandhi was posthumously granted the freedom of the city in Pietermaritzburg in April 1997. The statue was unveiled in 1993 by Archbishop Desmond Tutu and it is known as the Statue of Peace.

BOTANICAL GARDENS & HOWICK FALLS


The city is not known as the city of flowers for nothing. There is a Botanical Gardens that is filled with lush vegetaation and seasonal blooms.The garden was established in the early 1870s and essentially focus on the conservation and propagation of rare and indigenious plants and cultivation of east coast grasslands. The garden is famous for its long avenues of plane trees planted in 1908.

Howick Falls are located in Howick outside Pietermaritzburg and provides a spectacular view. The falls are about 95m in height, about the same height as the Victoria Fall of Zimbabwe. It is believed that the region remains unchanged for the last 300 centuries. Though Howick Falls on the Mngeni River may not boast the inclusion in the list of tallest waterfalls , they can provide some interest to thode morbidly inclined.
