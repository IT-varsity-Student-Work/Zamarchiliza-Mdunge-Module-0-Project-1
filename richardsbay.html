RICHARDSBAY

Richardsbay is located on a 30 square kilometre lagoon of Mhlathuze River, which gives it one of the country's largest harbours. Richardsbay also has the deepes natural harbours in the African continent. It began as a makeshift habour that was set up by Commodore of the Cape during the Anglo-Zulu War of 1879. In 1935 the Richardsbay Game Santuary was created to protect the ecology around the lagoon and later by 1943 it expanded into Richards Bay Park. In 1976 Richards Bay harbour was converted into a deep water harbour with railway and an oil pipeline linking the port to Johannesburg.


PORT OF RICHARDS BAY

The Port of Richards Bay contains what was once the largest coal export facility in the world, with a planned carpacity of 91 million tons per year by the first half of the year 2009. A fertiliser plant operated by Foskor has been erected at the habour. Iron ore, rutile and zicron are mined from the sand dunes close to the lagoon by Richard Bay Minerals. Local exports include coal, alaminium, titanium, and other hgeavy memirals, granite, ferrochrome, paper pulp, wood chips and phosphoric acid. Richards Bay is a fast-growing industrial centre that has been able to maintain its ecological diversity
